Open Wi-Finder was built for the Esri Canada Centres of Excellence 2015 App Challenge.

Purpose

  • The Esri Canada App Challenge is an annual activity among students studying at higher education institutes that are members of the Esri Canada Centres of Excellence program. The purpose of the challenge is to engage up to a maximum of three teams of participating students with a maximum of three members per team from each centre to work collaboratively on building a new app within a fixed time period using Esri, and other, components as required.
  • The primary objective of the challenge is, as the word suggests, to challenge students to use their technical GIS knowledge, creativity, and ability to innovate and work together to produce a relevant and functional app using the Esri platform. Realization of this objective will produce a number of new apps that showcase the abilities of students studying at the centres of excellence. A secondary objective is to contribute to building a strong sense of community among senior undergraduate and graduate students studying at Esri Canada Centres of Excellence. Realization of this objective will allow students to ‘see’ beyond their own school into the broader landscape of GIScience in higher education across Canada.

Task

Use open spatial data to develop an app on some aspect (delivery, access, quality, maintenance, impact, planning etc.) of Government (municipal and/or regional) Services in Canada for any of the following themes:

  • Housing
  • Health (human and/or animal/environmental)
  • Parks and recreation
  • Education including daycare
  • Public/private transport including infrastructure
  • Garbage collection/snow/branch removal
  • Emergency management
  • Information services (online information access and use)
  • Utilities.
